Traditional South African trifle

Description

South Africans love dessert after big feasts as much as everybody else and it is the season of no-guilt eating. There’s one dessert that has many variations and arguments about what the right version is and it’s trifle. It’s a tall layered dessert which has biscuits, jelly and custard in each layer. Our South African trifle recipe has all of the landmarks of this iconic dessert with an Amarula cream liqueur twist.

Additional info

You want a deep bowl for this tall dessert as each layer is stacked high. The Amarula creaminess is great with the ladyfinger or cat’s tongue biscuits but you can also soak them in brandy or sherry. You can of course make your own homemade custard, however, sometimes it’s easier to use the store-bought version which everybody enjoys and it’s a bit thicker and will hold its shape. The fruit and jelly flavour choices are also up to you and you can stack your trifle as high as you want to.

Ingredients

  • 150 ml Amarula Cream Liqueur
  • 250 ml fresh cream
  • 1 packet ladyfinger biscuits
  • 1 tin caramel treat (filling and topping)
  • 2 packets jelly powder (different flavours)
  • fresh fruit of your choice
  • chopped nuts
  • 1½ liter custard

Directions

  1. Prepare the jelly according to package instructions and allow to set.
  2. Place the ladyfinger biscuits at the bottom of a deep glass bowl.
  3. Moisten the ladyfinger biscuits with the Amarula Cream Liqueur.
  4. Spread the Caramel Treat over the biscuits.
  5. Chop up the jelly and add over the caramel-spread biscuits.
  6. Add the fruit and then the custard.
  7. Keep layering until you’ve used all the ingredients.
  8. Cover with whipped cream and decorate with nuts.
